Problem

Our promotion coordinator needed to know what items sold best, and when. My job was to compile sales data into a format that she could use. However, the task was both time-consuming and inefficient.

Solution

Because our promotion coordinator needed to merge sales data with other information, she needed the ability to not just view a list of recommendations, but to explore further. What we needed was something that was not static but interactive. We needed a tool, not just a spreadsheet. What we needed was Tableau.


Before switching to Tableau, I would easily spend 3-4 hours just compiling the data and then formatting in an Excel workbook. Follow-up requests for clarifications or additional data could add another hour of work, depending on the level of detail required. Tableau took the tedium out of the task. After switching, all I had to do was refresh the data and export the workbook to a network location for pickup.


The dashboard also enabled deeper analysis. Its interactivity and advanced charting features made it possible to display year-over-year trends in a meaningful way. Packaging related data in the same workbook also became easier, enabling us to display and analyze the terms that people use to search our website.

Resolution

All said and done, one Tableau dashboard saved us 36+ hours per year by simplifying this one task. Aside from providing benefits to my own team, personnel in other departments expressed an interest in the dashboard and began to use it for their own analyses.

Problem

I identified several optimizations in the course of my SEO audit. Repairing broken Schema was one of those. However, the dev team manager needed more details to verify whether the value would offset the cost.

Solution

The Schema portion of the SEO audit that I performed only summarized the benefits of optimization. Because a non-trivial amount of labor could potentially be involved, I needed to estimate the potential sales lift that this task could have.


Because Schema would have a direct impact on our search results, estimating an increased click-through rate (CTR) seemed like the best method. Using search queries pulled from Google Search Console and Google Analytics, I calculated the traffic that would result from an increased CTR and applied per-session revenue to get the value of the optimization.

Resolution

Using the performance of a few of our strongest keywords as a base, I estimated that repairing our Schema could lift monthly sales 10% – 30%. This satisfied the request for more detail, and the included implementation steps were deemed sufficient.

Problem

Prior to my arrival, The Company had never conducted an SEO audit. This made it difficult to measure progress, identify opportunities, and gain insight into the Company's web traffic.

Solution

The only way to rank well in search engine results these days is to retain strength across many areas of web design. This includes business listings, technical optimizations, and backlinks. Therefore, an SEO audit would need to be comprehensive to provide any lasting value.


Using metrics provided by various Google resources, as well as the Company's internal data, I benchmarked the Company's performance using KPIs that Google lists as ranking factors and calculated the value of optimizations that the Company could implement.

Resolution

Estimating the value of SEO for the Company was a nuanced process. In the end, I opted to use conservative estimates that excluded phone/email transactions. In this model, optimizations were worth approximately $169k annually. Because many of them were rather technical, they were broken out into their own projects to make implementation more manageable.
